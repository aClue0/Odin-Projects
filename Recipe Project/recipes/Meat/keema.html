Keema

Description

Keema is a traditional South Asian dish made with minced meat, typically lamb or beef, cooked with a blend of spices and herbs. The name "keema" is derived from the Persian word "qeyma," which means minced meat.

Keema is a versatile dish that can be served in various ways, such as with rice, naan bread, or as a filling for samosas or parathas. The dish is known for its rich and flavorful taste, which is achieved through the use of spices like cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala. Keema is often garnished with fresh cilantro and served with a side of yogurt or raita to balance the spiciness.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds ground lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons garam masala
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 4 teaspoons tomato paste
  • ¾ cup beef broth

Steps

  • Cook lamb in a large, heavy skillet over medium heat until browned and crumbly, 5 to 7 minutes. Use a slotted spoon to transfer lamb to a bowl; drain off all but 1 tablespoon fat from the skillet.
  • Add onion to the skillet and sauté until soft and translucent, about 5 minutes. Stir in garlic and cook until fragrant, about 1 minute. Stir in garam masala and salt; cook for 1 minute. Return browned lamb to the pan with broth and tomato paste. Reduce the heat and simmer until lamb is fully cooked through and liquid has evaporated, 10 to 15 minutes.
